## Growth Experiments Overview

**Purpose:** Test different growth strategies to identify what works best for Settler.

**Approach:** Hypothesis-driven experimentation with clear success metrics.

**Process:**
1. Formulate hypothesis
2. Design experiment
3. Run experiment
4. Measure results
5. Analyze and iterate

---

## Growth Experiments (10+)

### Experiment 1: Product Hunt Launch

**Hypothesis:** Launching on Product Hunt will drive 1,000+ signups and 100+ paying customers.

**Experiment:**
- Prepare Product Hunt launch (landing page, demo video, early supporters)
- Launch on Product Hunt
- Engage with community
- Track signups and conversions

**Metrics:**
- Product Hunt ranking
- Signups from Product Hunt
- Conversion rate (signup → paid)
- Revenue from Product Hunt

**Timeline:** Month 4  
**Status:** Planned

## Experiment Prioritization

### Prioritization Framework

**Impact vs. Effort Matrix:**

| Impact | Effort | Priority |
|--------|--------|----------|
| High | Low | P0: Do First |
| High | High | P1: Do Next |
| Low | Low | P2: Do Later |
| Low | High | P3: Don't Do |

**Factors:**
- Potential impact (signups, revenue)
- Effort required (time, resources)
- Risk level
- Strategic alignment

## Experiment Best Practices

### Do's

- ✅ Formulate clear hypotheses
- ✅ Define success metrics
- ✅ Run experiments systematically
- ✅ Document results and learnings
- ✅ Iterate based on results

### Don'ts

- ❌ Run experiments without hypotheses
- ❌ Skip measurement
- ❌ Ignore results
- ❌ Run too many experiments at once
- ❌ Forget to document learnings
