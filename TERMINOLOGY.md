# Settler Terminology Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2024-01-01

This document defines consistent terminology used across all Settler documentation, marketing materials, and communications.

## Product Names

### Settler API
- **Full Name:** Settler API (or Settler SaaS)
- **Description:** Proprietary reconciliation-as-a-service API
- **License:** Proprietary (Closed Source)
- **Pricing:** Usage-based + subscription tiers
- **Use Case:** Production businesses needing reconciliation infrastructure

### React.Settler
- **Full Name:** React.Settler (or @settler/react-settler)
- **Description:** Open-source React component library for reconciliation UIs
- **License:** MIT (OSS) + Commercial License (Commercial Features)
- **Pricing:** Free (OSS) / $99/month (Commercial) / Custom (Enterprise)
- **Use Case:** Developers building reconciliation UIs declaratively

**Note:** Always use "React.Settler" (with dot) in formal documentation. Can use "@settler/react-settler" when referring to npm package.

## License Tiers

### OSS Tier
- **Full Name:** Open Source (OSS) Tier
- **Alternative Names:** Free Tier, OSS License
- **License:** MIT License
- **Pricing:** Free Forever
- **Features:** Core protocol, basic components, security basics

**Usage:** "OSS tier", "open-source tier", "free tier" (all acceptable)

### Commercial Tier
- **Full Name:** Commercial Tier
- **Alternative Names:** Commercial License, Commercial Subscription
- **License:** Commercial License Agreement
- **Pricing:** $99/month or $990/year
- **Features:** Platform integrations, virtualization, telemetry, audit logging

**Usage:** "Commercial tier", "commercial license", "commercial subscription" (all acceptable)

### Enterprise Tier
- **Full Name:** Enterprise Tier
- **Alternative Names:** Enterprise License, Enterprise Subscription
- **License:** Enterprise License Agreement
- **Pricing:** Custom ($5K-$50K+/year)
- **Features:** SSO, RBAC, white-label, dedicated support

**Usage:** "Enterprise tier", "enterprise license", "enterprise subscription" (all acceptable)

## Feature Categories

### Core Features
- **Definition:** Essential features available in all tiers
- **Includes:** Protocol types, basic components, config compiler, validation, security basics
- **Tier:** OSS (Free)

### Commercial Features
- **Definition:** Advanced features requiring Commercial subscription
- **Includes:** Platform integrations, virtualization, telemetry, audit logging
- **Tier:** Commercial ($99/month)

### Enterprise Features
- **Definition:** Enterprise-specific features requiring Enterprise subscription
- **Includes:** SSO, RBAC, custom integrations, white-label
- **Tier:** Enterprise (Custom)

## Business Terms

### ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
- **Definition:** Total annual revenue from subscriptions
- **Usage:** "$170K ARR (Year 1)"

### MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- **Definition:** Total monthly revenue from subscriptions
- **Usage:** "$10K MRR"

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- **Definition:** Cost to acquire a new customer
- **Usage:** "CAC: $300 average"

### LTV (Lifetime Value)
- **Definition:** Total revenue from a customer over their lifetime
- **Usage:** "LTV: $15,000 average"

### NRR (Net Revenue Retention)
- **Definition:** Revenue retention including expansion
- **Usage:** "NRR: 110%+"

### Churn
- **Definition:** Rate of customer cancellations
- **Usage:** "Monthly churn: <5%"

## Customer Segments

### Developers
- **Definition:** Individual developers or small teams
- **Journey:** OSS → Commercial
- **Typical Spend:** $0-$99/month

### Growing Businesses
- **Definition:** E-commerce platforms, SaaS companies, marketplaces (10-100 employees)
- **Journey:** Commercial → Enterprise
- **Typical Spend:** $99-$499/month

### Enterprise
- **Definition:** Large organizations (500+ employees)
- **Journey:** Enterprise sales process
- **Typical Spend:** $5K-$50K+/year

## Sales Funnel Stages

### Awareness
- **Definition:** Top of funnel, discovery phase
- **Metrics:** GitHub stars, npm downloads, website traffic

### Consideration
- **Definition:** Middle of funnel, evaluation phase
- **Metrics:** Documentation views, playground usage, community engagement

### Evaluation
- **Definition:** Bottom of funnel, trial phase
- **Metrics:** Trial signups, OSS projects, demos

### Purchase
- **Definition:** Conversion phase
- **Metrics:** Signups, MRR, conversion rate

### Expansion
- **Definition:** Retention and growth phase
- **Metrics:** NRR, expansion revenue, retention rate

## Technical Terms

### Reconciliation
- **Definition:** Process of matching records between systems
- **Usage:** "Payment reconciliation", "reconciliation workflow"

### Transaction
- **Definition:** Individual payment or financial record
- **Usage:** "Reconciliation transaction", "transaction table"

### Settlement
- **Definition:** Funds settled to merchant account
- **Usage:** "Settlement record", "transaction-settlement matching"

### Exception
- **Definition:** Record requiring manual review
- **Usage:** "Reconciliation exception", "exception handling"

### Rule
- **Definition:** Matching rule for reconciliation
- **Usage:** "Reconciliation rule", "matching rule"

### Community Support

- **Definition:** 24/7 support with dedicated account manager

## Legal Terms

### MIT License
- **Definition:** Open-source license (free, permissive)
- **Usage:** "Licensed under MIT License"

### Commercial License
- **Definition:** Paid license for commercial features
- **Usage:** "Requires Commercial License"

### Enterprise Agreement
- **Definition:** Custom contract for Enterprise tier
- **Usage:** "Governed by Enterprise Agreement"

## Consistent Usage Guidelines

1. **Always use:** "React.Settler" (with dot) in formal docs
2. **Always use:** "Settler API" (not "Settler SaaS" in customer-facing docs)
3. **Always use:** "OSS tier" or "Commercial tier" (not "free tier" or "paid tier")
4. **Always use:** "$99/month" (not "$99/mo" or "$99 per month")
5. **Always use:** "Commercial License" (not "Pro License" or "Premium License")
6. **Always use:** "Enterprise tier" (not "Enterprise plan" or "Enterprise edition")
